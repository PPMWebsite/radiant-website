Major Servicing

Compressor Overhaul & Reconditioning

When routine servicing isn't enough — when performance has deteriorated, unusual noises have developed, or a major failure has occurred — a full overhaul restores your compressor to working condition and extends its service life significantly. Often a fraction of the cost of replacement.

What It Is

A Full Mechanical Rebuild — Not Just a Service

An overhaul goes far beyond routine maintenance. It involves stripping the compressor down to its major components, inspecting every part, replacing worn or failed items, and rebuilding the unit to manufacturer specification.

This is the right intervention when a compressor shows signs of declining performance — reduced pressure delivery, increased oil consumption, overheating, excessive noise or vibration — or after a serious mechanical failure. A properly executed overhaul can restore a compressor to near-new operating condition and extend its useful life by many years.

Radiant Engineering carries out overhauls on all major brands. We assess the scope before committing to a fixed quote, so you know exactly what the work involves and what you'll get back.

Who It’s For

When an overhaul is the right call

  • Compressors showing declining performance — reduced pressure, lower flow, higher specific power, or increased oil carry-over.
  • Post-breakdown units with major internal damage — motor failure, airend seizure, or severe contamination that requires full disassembly.
  • End-of-warranty major service — many manufacturers recommend a full overhaul at 20,000–40,000 hours to reset the service clock.
  • Where replacement cost is prohibitive — a quality overhaul typically costs 30–50% of a new unit and delivers comparable reliability.

Overhaul Scope

What’s Included in an Overhaul

Every overhaul starts with a full assessment. Final scope depends on the unit's condition — we'll confirm before any work begins.

  • Full strip-down and condition assessment — complete disassembly with documented inspection findings and photographic record before work begins
  • Airend overhaul — screw element or piston inspection, rotor bearing replacement, shaft seal kits, and clearance verification
  • Bearing and seal replacement — all main drive bearings, shaft seals, and gaskets replaced regardless of apparent condition
  • Cooler cleaning and pressure test — oil cooler and aftercooler descaled, flushed, and hydraulically tested to confirm integrity
  • Controller and electrical inspection — check control board, temperature and pressure sensors, solenoid valves, motor winding resistance
  • Valve gear and inlet valve overhaul — inlet valve, minimum pressure valve, and thermostatic bypass valve serviced or replaced as required
  • Full consumables replacement — new oil separator, oil filter, air filter, and compressor oil fitted on reassembly
  • Post-overhaul performance test — run-in period, pressure and temperature verification, and leak check before redelivery or recommissioning on-site

Common Questions

FAQ

What is the difference between repair and overhaul?

Repair addresses specific faults or failures, while overhaul focuses on restoring overall compressor performance by addressing multiple worn or degraded components.

When should an air compressor be overhauled?

Overhaul is typically considered when breakdowns become frequent, performance declines, or repair costs increase without delivering long-term stability.

Is overhaul more cost-effective than replacing a compressor?

In some cases, overhaul can extend the usable life of a compressor at a lower cost than full replacement. Suitability depends on condition, usage, and operating requirements.

How long does an overhaul usually take?

Overhaul timelines vary depending on compressor type, condition, and scope of work. We will advise expected timeframes after assessment.

Can overhaul improve compressor performance?

Yes. Proper overhaul and reconditioning can restore operating stability and improve reliability compared to a compressor that has been running in a degraded state.
