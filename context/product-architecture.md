Product Architecture Goal:
Use products as a stronger commercial and SEO driver than the current live site.

Primary Approach:
- Build category landing pages, not individual product pages as the starting structure
- Allow sub-category sections within category pages where useful
- Focus on discoverability, credibility, and enquiry generation rather than deep ecommerce functionality

Top-Level Product Structure:
- Ceccato (Italy)
- Compressor Accessories
- Spare Parts
- View All Products

Known Sub-Category Structure:
Ceccato (Italy)
- CSM Series
- DRB Series - Variable Speed

Page Strategy:
- Each top-level category should be able to function as its own landing page
- Category pages should explain what the category is, who it is for, key use cases, and how to enquire
- Sub-categories can be shown as cards, sections, or anchor links within the parent category page
- Products should support both SEO and lead generation, not just catalog display

Commercial Intent:
- Make products more visible than on the current live site
- Use products to attract higher-margin enquiries
- Make it easy for visitors to move from product browsing into WhatsApp, call, or quote action

SEO Intent:
- Build category pages that can target industrial product search intent
- Use clear headings, category descriptions, use cases, supported brands/series where factual, and internal linking to service pages
- Avoid thin category pages with no explanatory content

UX Intent:
- Product categories should be easy to scan
- Use card-based category entry points
- Repeat CTA options throughout category pages
- Keep browsing simple on mobile

Content Rules:
- Keep factual claims aligned with the current live websites
- Do not invent product specifications, stock levels, certifications, or brand relationships
- Where detailed specs are unavailable, keep wording practical and general
